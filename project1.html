Overview

The iNM project endeavors to craft user-friendly mobile apps by leveraging the knowledge of UI/UX, web design, and graphic design acquired during previous lectures.

Highlight

Role Contribution & Responsibility:

Within the collaborative framework of the iNM School App project, my specific responsibility centered on the creation of the Splash Screen and Home Screen.

Crafting the initial visual impact with the Splash Screen required a meticulous blend of design elements to captivate users from the outset. Simultaneously, shaping the Home Screen involved thoughtful consideration of layout and functionality to ensure an intuitive and engaging user experience.

This dual role not only honed my design skills but also heightened my awareness of the critical role these screens play in setting the tone for user interaction. The challenges and triumphs faced in executing these components underscored the importance of seamless integration within the broader context of the app, contributing to a holistic understanding of UI/UX design.

Brainstorming Process

Engaging in open dialogue and brainstorming sessions, we fostered the exchange of opinions within the team. We harnessed tools like Google Document Sharing and color palette sites to facilitate our creative process. Diverse app concepts were thrown into the mix, ranging from food nutrients and local restaurants to pets, alongside school-related ideas. Eventually, we reached a unanimous decision to develop a new school app, a common ground that addressed the inconveniences we had all experienced.

Concepts and Drafts

Drawing inspiration from our brainstorming endeavors, we distilled the iNM app's core tasksâ€”logo creation, style guide development, and prototype design. Additionally, we identified five key features: notifications, store, marketplace, student wallet, and community. These elements served as the foundation for the creation of approximately seven screen configurations, which were ultimately incorporated into the final version of our prototype.

Design & Configuration

Amassing information for the app proved relatively facile, as we already possessed extensive knowledge on the chosen topic. Our initial step involved studying existing competitive apps, dissecting their common features, and pinpointing areas for enhancement. The real challenge lay in organizing and harmonizing the screen layouts in adherence to design principles. Given my ongoing journey with Figma, it took me considerable time to locate and implement the desired features effectively.

Prototype Completion

In an effort to ensure a comprehensive design and seamless integration of content and visuals, each team member was assigned two screens for scrutiny and discussion. This collaborative approach enabled us to fine-tune the final design, skillfully combining images created using Adobe Illustrator and modified using Photoshop. The primary objective of iNM remains to heighten user convenience by addressing existing app issues and incorporating indispensable functionalities, thereby elevating the overall utilization rate of our applications.

Reflection

Executing this project in an agile manner, where each team member actively contributed from inception to completion, provided a hands-on experience in the collaborative dynamics of UI/UX design. The emphasis on communication among team members became glaringly evident, underscoring its pivotal role in project success. Through the process of designing the app, I gained a profound appreciation for the intricacies involved in addressing user inconveniences. Contrary to initial assumptions, it proved to be a nuanced challenge, prompting deeper contemplation on the intricacies of enhancing user experience. This undertaking not only refined my technical skills but also heightened my awareness of the multifaceted nature of effective collaboration and the complexities inherent in crafting user-centric solutions.
